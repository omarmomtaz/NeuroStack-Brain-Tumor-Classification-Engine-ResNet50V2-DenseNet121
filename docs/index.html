The Problem

Brain tumors are among the most lethal malignancies in human medicine. Glioma — a diffuse, aggressive infiltrator — and Meningioma — a typically benign but surgically complex mass — demand radically different clinical pathways.

Misclassification is not a metric failure. It is a life-altering event. A missed glioma delays chemotherapy. A false glioma diagnosis triggers unnecessary neurosurgical intervention.

Radiologists face fatigue, workload, and inter-reader variability that human cognition cannot consistently overcome. AI does not get tired.

"The goal was not to build a model that performs well. The goal was to build a model that a clinician could trust."

Phase 01
Dataset Acquisition
2,800 training + 800 test MRI scans. Masoud Nickparvar's curated Brain Tumor MRI Dataset. Balanced classes — 1,400 Meningioma, 1,400 Glioma.
Phase 02
Preprocessing Pipeline
Custom BrainMRIPreprocessor with augmentation, MixUp training, and standardized 224×224 crops. Zero data leakage enforced across all 5 stratified folds.
Phase 03
Architecture Design
10 base models trained in parallel. ResNet50V2 and DenseNet121, each across 5 cross-validation folds. Logistic Regression meta-learner stacked on top.

System Architecture

NeuroStack operates as a two-level stacked ensemble. Ten independent CNN base models each produce probability distributions. Those are concatenated into a 20-dimensional feature vector, fed to a trained meta-learner that synthesizes the final verdict.

Stacked Ensemble

  • StandardScaler applied
  • Logistic Regression

DESIGN CHOICE 01
Stacking over Voting

Simple majority voting discards rich probability information. A trained meta-learner learns how much to trust each model and in what scenarios — a fundamentally superior aggregation strategy.

DESIGN CHOICE 02
Heterogeneous Architectures

ResNet's residual skip connections and DenseNet's feature reuse provide complementary inductive biases. Their correlation of 0.94–0.99 confirms agreement while their failure modes differ, enabling ensemble gains.

DESIGN CHOICE 03
MixUp Training

Convex combinations of training pairs force the model to learn smoother decision boundaries rather than memorizing specific MRI textures. Proven to reduce overconfidence and improve generalization in medical imaging.
